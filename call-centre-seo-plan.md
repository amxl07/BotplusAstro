# BotPlus AI Contact Centre SEO & Go-To-Market Plan

## 1. Executive Recommendation

### Recommendation

Launch the call centre product as a serious standalone product with its own domain, but keep BotPlus as the parent-company authority site.

The product should not live only as a section on the current BotPlus website. The opportunity is large enough, competitive enough, and global enough to justify a dedicated product brand, product website, SEO architecture, conversion funnel, and sales motion.

### Best structure

| Asset | Role |
|---|---|
| `botplus.tech` / current BotPlus website | Parent company, credibility, platform narrative, existing regional SEO capture |
| New product domain | Main global product site for AI contact centre SEO, lead generation, demos, ROI calculators, industry pages |
| BotPlus product page | Bridge page that introduces the product and links strongly to the new product domain |
| Product domain footer/about page | Link back to BotPlus as the platform/company behind the product |

### Why separate domain

The current site is platform-level and Middle East focused. The call centre opportunity is global, product-specific, and commercially large. A dedicated domain gives the product a cleaner category identity, avoids confusing buyers with avatars/AR/retail products, and lets the SEO strategy go deep on one market.

### Recommended positioning

> BotPlus AI Contact Centre / ResolveDesk helps enterprises and mid-market operators reduce repetitive support workload through scoped AI workflow automation across voice, WhatsApp, email, Messenger, and web.

External claim guidance:

```text
Use "target 30-50% reduction in scoped repetitive support workload" until measured customer pilots support stronger public claims.
```

Do not use 50-60% as a guaranteed website, ad, or outbound claim.

### Product category

**AI Contact Centre Automation Platform**

Secondary category terms:

- AI call center software
- AI contact center platform
- Voice AI for contact centers
- Customer service automation platform
- Omnichannel AI support platform

## 2. Domain Strategy

### Options

| Option | Pros | Cons | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Keep product under BotPlus, e.g. `/ai-call-center/` | Uses existing domain history, faster initial indexing, easier maintenance | Website remains broad and ME-focused, product identity weaker, SEO topical authority diluted | Good short-term bridge, not enough as long-term strategy |
| Subdomain, e.g. `contact.botplus.tech` | Brand connection, some separation | Subdomains often behave like separate sites for SEO, less clean than product domain | Not preferred |
| Separate product domain | Best global product positioning, clean topical authority, stronger brand/category fit | Starts with no domain authority, requires sustained content/link effort | Recommended if serious about global market |

### Final recommendation

Use a separate product domain as the primary global acquisition site.

Keep a small but strong product section on BotPlus that does three jobs:

1. Pass trust and link equity to the product domain.
2. Capture current UAE/Saudi/Middle East search impressions.
3. Explain that the product is powered by the BotPlus enterprise AI automation platform.

### Domain name guidance

Choose a name that can become a real product brand, not only a keyword domain.

Good naming patterns:

| Pattern | Example Style | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Product brand | `CallDeskAI`, `ContactDeskAI`, `VoiceDeskAI`, `SupportDeskAI` | Best for long-term brand building |
| Outcome brand | `SaveCX`, `ResolveAI`, `AlwaysAnswerAI` | Good if memorable and available |
| Category + brand | `BotPlusContact`, `BotPlusVoice`, `BotPlusCX` | Keeps parent connection but less independent |
| Exact-match keyword | `aicallcenter...` | SEO benefit is limited today; can feel generic |

Avoid names that are too narrow:

- Only “voice” if the product is omnichannel.
- Only “call” if the long-term product includes WhatsApp, email, ticketing, web, kiosks, and avatars.
- Only “bot” because enterprises may associate bots with low-quality chatbots.

Recommended naming direction:

> A product brand around **Contact**, **CX**, **Desk**, **Resolve**, **Support**, or **Answer**.

Example positioning if the product name is `ContactDesk AI`:

> ContactDesk AI, powered by BotPlus, is an AI contact centre automation platform for enterprises that want to reduce support cost without reducing service quality.

### Domain launch model

Do not duplicate the same pages on BotPlus and the new domain.

Use this model:

| Content | Lives On | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Full product SEO pages | New product domain | Canonical to themselves |
| Global industry pages | New product domain | Government, finance, retail, healthcare, utilities, logistics |
| Calculator | New product domain | Primary conversion asset |
| Blog cluster | New product domain | Build topical authority |
| Short product overview | BotPlus website | Links to product domain |
| Parent-company proof/platform page | BotPlus website | Explains BotPlus infrastructure |
| Case studies | Product domain first, BotPlus can summarize and link | Avoid duplicate full copies |

### Cross-linking rules

| From | To | Link Style |
|---|---|---|
| BotPlus homepage | BotPlus internal product bridge page | Navigation/card link |
| BotPlus products page | Product domain homepage or gateway | Strong CTA link |
| BotPlus AI call centre bridge page | Product domain gateway | Primary CTA |
| Product domain footer | BotPlus homepage/about | “Powered by BotPlus” trust link |
| Product domain about page | BotPlus platform page | Parent-company credibility link |
| Product domain blog | BotPlus rarely | Only when explaining parent platform, not routinely |

Important:

- The product domain should not canonicalize to BotPlus.
- BotPlus should not host duplicated long-form product pages.
- BotPlus should act as the parent and referral source.
- Product domain should build its own topical authority.

## 8. Market Analysis

### Market drivers

- Enterprises are moving from legacy contact centres to cloud and AI-enabled service operations.
- Customers expect 24/7 instant support.
- Support leaders need omnichannel continuity across voice, chat, WhatsApp, email, and social channels.
- CFOs and COOs are under pressure to reduce operating cost.
- Multilingual service is expensive to staff manually.
- Contact centre leaders want better analytics and visibility.
- AI adoption creates security, governance, and hallucination concerns that enterprise vendors must address.

### Market size context

Grand View Research estimates the global contact center software market at USD 47.71B in 2025, projected to reach USD 227.57B by 2033 with 21.9% CAGR. The fastest growth is expected in hosted/cloud deployments, SMEs, and Asia Pacific.

### Customer expectation context

Zendesk CX Trends 2026 reports that customers increasingly expect 24/7 support, faster response times, memory-rich interactions, multimodal support, and transparency from AI decisions.

### Security context

IBM's Cost of a Data Breach 2025 highlights the risk of ungoverned AI adoption, including lack of AI governance policies and access controls. This strengthens the need to sell BotPlus with governance, escalation, auditability, and enterprise data controls.

The two sites should work together like this:

```text
BotPlus site

  -> links to product domain as featured product

Product domain
  = global AI contact centre category site
  -> links back to BotPlus as parent/platform
```

This gives the company the best of both paths:

- Use existing BotPlus impressions and regional rankings now.
- Build a focused global product asset for the larger opportunity.
- Avoid diluting the product under a broad platform site.
